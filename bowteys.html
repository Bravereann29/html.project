Mental Health Conditions

Gain understanding of common mental health conditions, their symptoms, and available treatments.Many people have mental health concerns from time to time. But a mental health concern becomes a mental illness when ongoing signs and symptoms cause frequent stress and affect your ability to function.

  • Feeling sad or down
  • Confused thinking or reduced ability to concentrate
  • fears or worries, or extreme feelings of guilt
  • Extreme mood changes of highs and lows
  • Withdrawal from friends and activities
  • Significant tiredness, low energy or problems sleeping
  • Trouble understanding and relating to situations and to people

Find a Therapist

Connect with qualified mental health professionals in your area to receive personalized support.Mental health issues, whether anxiety, depression, trauma, or stress, can feel overwhelming. Therapists provide tools, strategies, and coping mechanisms to help you manage your emotions and navigate life's challenges. Whether through Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT), or trauma-informed approaches, a therapist can offer specific techniques to help you reframe negative thinking, regulate emotions, and develop healthy coping skills.

  • Support for Coping and Healing
  • Personalized Care and Treatment
  • Support in Overcoming Resistance
  • Professional Expertise and Training
  • Structured Approach to Growth

WHEN TO SEE THE DOCTOR?

If you have any signs or symptoms of a mental illness, see your primary care provider or a mental health professional. Most mental illnesses don't improve on their own, and if untreated, a mental illness may get worse over time and cause serious problems.
